Explained Simply

What's Happening Here

This is the opening of a full-day conference at UCLA about how artificial intelligence is transforming science. Two organizers -- the director of a major math institute and the founder of a new nonprofit -- welcome the audience and set the stage for the day's conversations. They explain why this gathering is happening now and what they hope to accomplish.

IPAM (Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics): A research institute at UCLA funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF). Its mission is to bring together mathematicians with scientists, engineers, and industry professionals to collaborate on problems where math can make a difference. IPAM runs workshops and long-term programs -- think of it as a meeting ground where people from different fields come together to work on hard problems using mathematics.
S Foundation: A newly created charitable organization whose mission is "AI for science and science for AI." It aims to build bridges between academic researchers, industry, entrepreneurs, and philanthropists to ensure that AI development is scientifically grounded and benefits humanity broadly. This conference is one of its first public events.

Why This Matters

This event sits at a crossroads moment. AI capabilities are advancing so fast that the academic world, the tech industry, and government are all scrambling to figure out what it means for their work. Dimitri's point about IPAM hosting an AI-related program back in 2011 -- well before the current AI boom -- shows that mathematics has been intertwined with AI development for longer than most people realize. The fact that Yann LeCun (one of the "godfathers" of deep learning and a Turing Award winner) co-organized early programs at IPAM underscores the institute's deep roots in this space.

Chuck's framing of "AI for science and science for AI" captures a two-way relationship: AI tools can accelerate scientific discovery, but rigorous science is also needed to make AI itself more trustworthy and capable. The emphasis on building bridges -- between academia and industry, between different countries and disciplines -- reflects a growing consensus that the challenges and opportunities of AI are too big for any single institution or sector to tackle alone.

Key Insight: This conference represents a deliberate effort to break down the traditional walls between academic mathematics, scientific research, and the tech industry. The organizers believe that the AI moment is so significant that these communities must work together -- not just to use AI tools, but to shape how AI develops in a scientifically responsible way.
